Steve Irwin

Australian Wildlife Conservationist

Here is the time line of Steve Irwin's life:

  • 1962 - Born in Essendon, Victoria, Australia
  • 1970 - the family moved from Victoria to Queensland’s Sunshine Coast. This is when young Irwin accompanied his father on expeditions in the Outback to trap lizards, venomous snakes, and crocodiles
  • 1972 - Irwin first attempted to subdue a crocodile by jumping on its back. He became quite adept at the technique.
  • In the early 1980s - Irwin worked for the Crocodile Management Programon his own.
  • 1992 - The Cocodile Hunter, which is a 10-hours program, first aired in Australia, which featured Irwin in new adventures both inside and outside Australia.
  • 1996 - the program was picked up by the Discovery Channel network in United States.
  • September 2006 - a documentary called Ocean's Deadliest near Australia’s Great Barrier Reef was filming while Irwin swam over the a venomous stingray which inadvertently provoking the animal to attack. He was stung in the chest and immediately went into cardiac arrest, and he was pronounced dead less than an hour later.
